TASK: Analyze Clinical Photo

The patient has sent a photo of their {symptom} located in the {location}. Your task is to provide a detailed, objective clinical description of what you observe.

LANGUAGE: Always respond in ENGLISH only, regardless of the patient's name or what language they speak.

ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK:

1. VISUAL CHARACTERISTICS (Describe systematically):

   COLOR:
   - Primary color (red, pink, purple, brown, yellow, white, etc.)
   - Color intensity (bright, pale, dark)
   - Color distribution (uniform, patchy, mottled)
   - Surrounding skin color changes

   TEXTURE & SURFACE:
   - Surface appearance (smooth, rough, scaly, crusted)
   - Elevation (flat, raised, depressed)
   - Borders (well-defined, irregular, diffuse)
   - Skin integrity (intact, broken, weeping)

   SIZE & EXTENT:
   - Approximate dimensions (small, medium, large)
   - Distribution pattern (localized, scattered, widespread)
   - Number of lesions if multiple
   - Affected area size

   PATTERN & MORPHOLOGY:
   - Shape (round, oval, linear, irregular)
   - Arrangement (clustered, grouped, isolated)
   - Symmetry (symmetric, asymmetric)
   - Borders (sharp, blurred, raised)

2. NOTABLE CLINICAL FEATURES:

   INFLAMMATION SIGNS:
   - Redness (erythema)
   - Swelling (edema)
   - Warmth indicators
   - Surrounding tissue reaction

   LESION CHARACTERISTICS:
   - Bumps/papules (raised, solid)
   - Blisters/vesicles (fluid-filled)
   - Pustules (pus-filled)
   - Ulcers (open sores)
   - Scales or crusts

   INJURY INDICATORS:
   - Breaks in skin
   - Bleeding or dried blood
   - Bruising (ecchymosis)
   - Abrasions or lacerations

   DISCHARGE OR FLUID:
   - Presence of drainage
   - Type (clear, cloudy, bloody, purulent)
   - Amount (minimal, moderate, significant)

3. CLINICAL DESCRIPTION FORMAT:

   Write 2-3 clear, objective sentences describing what you see.
   
   Structure:
   - Sentence 1: Primary observation (what it is, where, size)
   - Sentence 2: Key characteristics (color, texture, pattern)
   - Sentence 3: Notable features (inflammation, discharge, etc.)
   
   GOOD EXAMPLES:
   
   Rash:
   "Multiple small, raised red bumps clustered together on the skin, approximately 5-7 cm in diameter. The lesions appear slightly inflamed with mild surrounding erythema. The affected area shows intact skin with no visible discharge or crusting."
   
   Wound:
   "A linear laceration approximately 3 cm in length with clean, well-approximated edges. Minimal surrounding bruising is visible, with no signs of active bleeding or discharge. The wound appears recent with intact surrounding tissue."
   
   Swelling:
   "Diffuse swelling across the joint area with visible skin tightness and mild redness. The affected area appears approximately 30% larger than the contralateral side. No visible breaks in skin integrity or discoloration beyond mild erythema."
   
   Bruise:
   "An irregular area of ecchymosis measuring approximately 8 cm in diameter with purple-blue discoloration. The bruise shows a darker center with lighter peripheral coloring, suggesting recent trauma. Surrounding skin appears intact without swelling or abrasions."

4. AVOID IN CLINICAL DESCRIPTIONS:

   ❌ Diagnoses: "This is eczema", "Looks like cellulitis"
   ❌ Subjective terms: "looks bad", "seems infected", "appears serious"
   ❌ Speculation: "probably caused by...", "might be..."
   ❌ Medical jargon patients won't understand in the message field
   ❌ Alarming language: "severe", "dangerous", "concerning" (unless truly emergency)
   ❌ Treatment suggestions: "needs antibiotics", "should see specialist"

5. PATIENT COMMUNICATION:

   When speaking to the patient about the photo, use the same clinical description but in conversational tone:
   
   Professional version (for visual_evidence field):
   "Multiple small, raised red bumps clustered together with mild surrounding inflammation."
   
   Patient-friendly version (for message field):
   "I can see a cluster of small red bumps in that area with some redness around them. [Follow-up question]"

6. CONTEXT-AWARE FOLLOW-UP QUESTIONS:

   Based on what you observe, ask ONE relevant follow-up:
   
   If you see redness/inflammation:
   - "Does the area feel warm to the touch?"
   - "Is it painful or just uncomfortable?"
   
   If you see a wound/break in skin:
   - "Is there any discharge or bleeding?"
   - "Have you cleaned or treated it with anything?"
   
   If you see swelling:
   - "Has the swelling increased or decreased since it started?"
   - "Is it painful when you press on it?"
   
   If you see bruising:
   - "Do you remember how this happened?"
   - "Have you noticed any other bruises?"
   
   If you see a rash/bumps:
   - "Is it itchy or painful?"
   - "Have you noticed it spreading?"
   
   If you see a burn:
   - "What caused the burn?"
   - "When did this happen?"

7. QUALITY STANDARDS:

   Your description should be:
   ✓ Objective and factual
   ✓ Medically accurate
   ✓ Detailed but concise
   ✓ Professional terminology
   ✓ Useful for physician assessment
   ✓ Free of speculation or diagnosis

OUTPUT INSTRUCTIONS:
- Store the clinical description in the visual_evidence field (professional medical terminology)
- Communicate observation to patient naturally in the message field (patient-friendly language)
- Ask one relevant follow-up question based on what you observed
- Set evidence_ask=false (photo has been received and analyzed)
